Big and new names sweep the Grammys

By Indra Dalaisaikhan

The 67th annual Grammy Awards, held on Feburary 2, 2025, at Los Angeles' Crypto.com Arena was a night of ground-breaking wins and performances. From big names like Béyonce winning Album of the Year and emerging acts like Chappell Roan taking her first Grammy for Best New Artist --- keep reading to discover the standout winners of the night.

Béyonce "COWBOY CARTER" - Album of the Year

With her latest LP, "COWBOY CARTER," Béyonce won her first Best Album of the Year nomination -- being the first Black woman to win the Grammy this century. Listen to her Grammy-winning album here:

Doechii "Alligator Bites Never Heal" - Best Rap Album

Doechii has been in the game for years with global hits like “Yucky Blucky Fruitcake” and “What It Is (Block Boy)." With her rising popularity in 2024 due to her latest album, “Alligator Bites Never Heal,” Doechii became the third woman in history to win the Best Rap Album at the Grammys.

Standing beside her mother on stage, Doechii delivered a heartfelt speech. After expressing gratitude to her label and others, she turned her attention to the Black women in the audience witnessing her historic win, saying:

"I know there’s a Black girl out there, so many Black women out there, watching me right now, and I want to tell you—you can do it. Anything is possible. Anything is possible."

Doechii continued to encourage Black women to stay true to themselves and believe that anything is possible. Her Grammy win being a "testement" to her words. (Audio credits to the Recording Academy / GRAMMYs.)
